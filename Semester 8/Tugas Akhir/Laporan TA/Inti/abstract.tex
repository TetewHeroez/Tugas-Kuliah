Max-plus algebra is an algebraic structure over $\mathbb{R}_{\max}$ equipped with the operations $a\oplus b=\max\{a,b\}$ and $a\otimes b=a+b$. One of the important problems in max-plus algebra is determining commutative pairs of matrices under multiplication. This research discusses the commutativity of two \textit{Latin squares} under max-plus multiplication. The \textit{Latin squares} studied are of order $n$ with the symbol set $\underline{n}=\{1,2,\ldots,n\}$, such that each of their rows and columns is a permutation of $\underline{n}$.

This research aims to determine the necessary conditions, sufficient conditions, and construction procedures for commutative \textit{Latin square} pairs. Each \textit{Latin square} is represented through a permutation matrix decomposition, namely $A=\bigoplus_{i=1}^{n} i\otimes P_{\sigma_i^A}$. Through this representation, the commutativity $A\otimes B=B\otimes A$ is analyzed based on the composition of the constituent permutations of $A$ and $B$. The results show that the necessary condition for commutativity can be obtained from the maximum elements, meaning the permutations of the maximum symbol must commute with each other. Furthermore, sufficient conditions are obtained through abelian subgroups of $S_n$, as well as necessary and sufficient criteria through the equality of the superlevel sets $\mathcal{U}_{\ge v}^{AB}$ and $\mathcal{U}_{\ge v}^{BA}$ for each $v\in\{n+2,n+3,\ldots,2n\}$.

Based on these criteria, an algorithm is developed to find a \textit{Latin square} $B$ that commutes with a given \textit{Latin square} $A$. The algorithm contains an alternative stage by rearranging the constituent permutations of $A$ when these permutations are commutative to one another, as well as a general stage utilizing the centralizer of the maximum symbol permutation, a partial candidate matrix $B^*$, and a step-by-step examination of the superlevel sets. The application of the algorithm on orders $3$, $4$, and $5$ demonstrates that the procedure can be used to obtain candidate pairs of commutative \textit{Latin squares}.
